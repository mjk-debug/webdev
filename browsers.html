The Rise of Web Browsers

The first browser, WorldWideWeb.app, was text-based and created by Tim Berners-Lee. In 1993, the revolutionary graphical browser Mosaic introduced images alongside text, enabling broader access to the Web.

Mosaic’s success led to the launch of Netscape Navigator in 1994, known for its user-friendly interface. However, the “browser wars” began when Microsoft released Internet Explorer in 1995, bundled with Windows, dominating for years.

In 2004, Mozilla Firefox brought innovations like tabbed browsing and better security. Google Chrome launched in 2008 and became the dominant browser due to its speed, minimal design, and frequent updates.

Netscape Navigator was one of the earliest successful web browsers

Today, browsers continue to evolve, with support for modern standards, mobile optimization, and advanced developer tools. Popular browsers include Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari, and Opera.
