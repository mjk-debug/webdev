Origins of the World Wide Web

The World Wide Web was envisioned by British computer scientist Sir Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 while working at CERN. At the time, global researchers struggled to share data across incompatible systems. Berners-Lee proposed a system using hypertext to interlink information. His proposal titled Information Management: A Proposal marked the birth of the web.

By 1990, Berners-Lee developed three foundational technologies:

  • HTML – For structuring web content
  • HTTP – For transferring data
  • URI – For locating resources (now commonly known as URLs)

He also created the first browser and web server, launching the first website in 1991 hosted at CERN.
